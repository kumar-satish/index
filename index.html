I am Satish Kumar, PhD student in Dr Rami Bahsoon's Research Lab at School of Computer Science, University of Birmingham, Birmingham, UK. I am fortunate to work under the supervision of Dr Rami Bahsoon, Dr Tao Chen (Loughborough University) and Prof. Rajkumar Buyya ( The University of Melbourne & Rank #1 Computer Scientist in Australia). In my PhD project, I am working towards developing a debt-aware economic driven framework for dynamic service composition in the SaaS Cloud. In particular, the ultimate goal of this research work is to create economic-driven approaches for understanding, improving and assessing the runtime behaviour of the Service-Based Software Systems. read more...

Research

Service Composition in SaaS Cloud using Technical Debt Analysis

Service composition is a key technology for building software application by composing multiple existing web services in SaaS Cloud. However, a software application in multi-tenant SaaS cloud would inevitably operate under dynamic changes on the workload from the tenants, and thus it is not uncommon for the composition to encounter under-utilisation and over-utilisation on the component services. The former reduces the service revenue and the latter, in contrast to the over-utilisation that leads to QoS constraints violation. In fact, both cases are undesirable, and bring a challenging task: when to (re)compose the component services such that the utility over time is maximised ?. read more...
